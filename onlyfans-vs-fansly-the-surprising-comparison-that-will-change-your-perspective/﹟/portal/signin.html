OnlyFans vs. Fansly: The Surprising Comparison That Will Change Your Perspective

In the world of adult content platforms, two major players have risen to prominence: OnlyFans and Fansly. In this article, we'll dive deep into the differences between these two platforms and answer some critical questions that creators often ask. We will discuss the potential for making more money on one platform over the other, the idea of having a backup account, and whether creators should be worried about the future of OnlyFans.

Features and Usability: Fansly Takes the Lead

In terms of features and usability, Fansly has the edge over OnlyFans. In fact, most of OnlyFans' competitors offer better technology, user experience, and overall features. However, this is not the main focus of our comparison today. We will concentrate on the factors that matter most to creators: making money, having a backup account, and the future of OnlyFans.

Can You Make More Money on Fansly?

To answer this question, we need to consider the number of users each platform has. According to SimilarWeb, OnlyFans has around 340 million users per month, while Fansly has about 29 million. The significant difference in user base plays a critical role in determining where a creator can make more money.

For instance, when promoting your content on social media platforms, the chances are higher that your potential subscribers already have an OnlyFans account. This means it's easier for them to become a subscriber with just one click. On the other hand, the probability of users already having a Fansly account is much lower. This means potential subscribers need to create a new account, input their credit card information, and complete the sign-up process, which can result in fewer conversions.

As long as OnlyFans maintains its massive user base advantage, creators will likely make more money on the platform.

Is Having a Fansly Account as a Backup a Good Idea?

Some creators might think that splitting their efforts between OnlyFans and Fansly as a backup strategy is a smart move. However, by dividing your focus and efforts, you could actually be limiting your potential earnings. For example, if you put 100% of your effort into promoting OnlyFans, you might make $100,000, while splitting your effort between the two platforms could result in earnings of only $65,000.

The bottom line is that focusing your energy on the platform with the highest potential return (in this case, OnlyFans) is the best strategy for maximizing your earnings.

Should You Be Worried About OnlyFans Dying?

It's essential to remember that your business is not OnlyFans itself, but your ability to gain attention and build an audience on social media. If OnlyFans were to disappear, its users would have to find an alternative platform, such as Fansly. In that scenario, you could simply redirect your audience to the new platform.

While the demise of OnlyFans might cause a temporary dip in your earnings, your true value lies in your ability to attract attention on social media and direct that attention to the platform of your choice.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while Fansly may offer better features and usability, OnlyFans holds the advantage in terms of user base and potential earnings. Creators should focus their efforts on the platform with the highest potential return and remember that their true business is their ability to build an audience on social media, not any specific platform.

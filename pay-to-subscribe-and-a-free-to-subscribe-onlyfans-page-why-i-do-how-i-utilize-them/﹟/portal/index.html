I Have a Pay-To-Subscribe AND a Free-To-Subscribe OnlyFans Page: Why I Do & How I Utilize Them

Today I am going to break down the difference between my paid-to-subscribe page and my free-to-subscribe pay-per-view only OnlyFans page. Many of you have asked me why I have the two different pages, what's the benefit of having one versus the other, and how do you set them up. In this article, I will discuss why I have the two separate OnlyFans pages and why it's beneficial. Let's dive in!

My Pay-To-Subscribe Page

My pay-to-subscribe page is my primary OnlyFans account, which I focus on 98% of the time. This is where I take all of my custom orders, do all my one-on-one messaging, and post daily content that is more risque directly in the feed for my fans to interact with. I also offer sexting and video chats, with the availability to book me for these services exclusively through my paid-to-subscribe OnlyFans page.

When I create new content, my paid subscribers receive discounts as a way to reward their loyalty. I take the time to personally respond to each and every fan who messages me. I believe this personal touch makes a significant difference and plays a huge part in the success of my page.

My Free-To-Subscribe Page

So, why did I start a free-to-subscribe page? It serves two main purposes: listing all of my pay-per-view content within the feed and hosting my live streams. I initially started this page because many of my paid subscribers were asking me about specific content, and it became time-consuming to answer each query individually. Now, I include a link to my free-to-subscribe page in my welcome message for paid subscribers, so they can easily access all of my pay-per-view content without having to ask me about it.

Not only does this save me time, but it's also a great way for me to make passive income on content I've already created. Another advantage of having a free-to-subscribe page is the ability to charge for access to live streams, which isn't possible on my paid-to-subscribe page.

Frequently Asked Questions About My Two Pages

Did I link my accounts? Unfortunately, OnlyFans is not perfect, and I've had trouble linking my free and paid accounts. I suspect this might be because one is pay-to-subscribe and the other is free-to-subscribe. As a workaround, I created two separate email addresses for my accounts. It's a bit of a hassle, but ultimately, it's not a big deal since all the money goes to the same bank account.

Conclusion

That's why I chose to have both a pay-to-subscribe page and a free-to-subscribe pay-per-view/live stream OnlyFans page. I hope this article has answered your questions and provided some clarity on the benefits of having two separate pages on the platform.
